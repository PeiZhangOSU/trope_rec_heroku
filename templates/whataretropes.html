What Are Tropes?

A trope is a reoccuring plot device. It can convey a concept to the audience without needing to spell out all the details. The usage of tropes is a short cut for describing situations the storyteller can reasonably assume the audience will recognize.

Here are some examples of tropes, with links to their pages on TVTropes.org, a site dedicated to cataloguing and studying tropes:

  • Heroic Sacrifice: A character saves another/others from harm and is killed, crippled, or maimed as a result.
  • Anti-Hero: An antihero is a protagonist who has the opposite of most of the traditional attributes of a hero. They may be bewildered, ineffectual, deluded, or merely apathetic.
  • Time Machine: A Time Machine is the main prerequisite for Time Travel and all the other fun that goes along with it.

    • The choice of tropes shapes where the story is going. And that is why I built this website -- it uses data to help you find the next best trope to add to your story.
